Children's Health Partners' Vaccination Policy - Effective June 1, 2017

The doctors of Children's Health Partners strongly believe the following:

  • Vaccinating your child may be the single most important thing we can do to help keep your child healthy.
  • It is our responsibility to protect as many children as possible from disease.
  • The recommended vaccines are safe.
  • Vaccines are extremely effective at preventing serious illness and saving lives.
  • Vaccines do not cause autism or other developmental disabilities.
  • The safety and effectiveness of vaccination is supported by an overwhelming amount of scientific research.
  • All children and adolescents should receive all of the recommended vaccinations according to the schedule published by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention and the American Academy of Pediatrics.
  • Vaccines are a victim of their own success. Most people have never seen a vaccine preventable disease. Because of this, some people are unaware of, or underestimate, the devastating complications of illnesses like meningitis and measles.
  • Open dialogue between the patient's family and doctor is critical regarding concerns about vaccination.
  • Splitting and delaying vaccinations allows children to unnecessarily go unprotected from preventable illness and death.

We are, however, sensitive to the concerns that many parents have regarding vaccination. Since the opening of our practice, the doctors of CHP have had thousands of discussions with parents with questions or concerns about vaccinations. Our goal has always been to develop long-term relationships with our patients and serve as a trusted resource where parents can feel comfortable asking any question about their child. For patients with vaccine concerns, we encourage you to show us the concerning articles or social media posts you have read about vaccination so that we may have an open and honest conversation about your fears. We also would like you to understand that we practice what we preach; all of us have vaccinated our own children according to the recommended AAP/CDC schedule. This includes HPV (human papilloma virus, a.k.a. the "cervical cancer vaccine") and yearly influenza vaccination.

With this as background we are announcing the following changes to our practice policy, effective June 1, 2017.

Children’s Health Partners is no longer accepting families who do not vaccinate their children.

For our current patients who are unvaccinated or undervaccinated for the DTaP, IPV, HIB, PCV13, Hep B, MMR, VZV (Chicken Pox), Tdap, or Menactra vaccines, we ask you to make an appointment with your child's physician to discuss vaccination. If you decide that vaccination is not an option for your family, we will have to ask you to find another health care provider for your child who shares your views. We have not taken this position to bully or coerce families into vaccination. We do this for three reasons:

  1. to emphasize the facts about immunization and how critical we feel vaccines are to your child’s health,
  2. to protect our patients who cannot be vaccinated (for medical reasons or because they are too young),
  3. to ensure that we are not passively supporting non-vaccination by allowing it to continue in our practice.

We would prefer to remain your child's pediatrician and will not dismiss any family who is actively working to vaccinate their child.

Children's Health Partners will no longer be completing the State of Illinois Certificate of Religious Exemption Forms.

If your child is unvaccinated or undervaccinated and has had a Well Child Visit at Children's Health Partners within the last year, then your child's school physical form and religious exemption form are good for one year from the date of their check-up. However, beginning June 1, 2017, we will no longer complete any State of Illinois Certificate of Religious Exemption forms associated with new Well Child Visits.

Frequently Asked Questions:

What if my child has a medical reason for which they can't be vaccinated?

Sometimes children have a medical reason for not being vaccinated, i.e. they are undergoing chemotherapy for cancer or are on immunosuppressants after an organ transplant. As long as patients receive the vaccines that are medically indicated, they are welcome at CHP.

What if I would like to space out my child's shots?

Spacing out immunizations leads to delays in infants being protected from preventable diseases. It is not a practice that we recommend. However, CHP will continue to offer this as an option, since it results in more children being immunized. We expect parents to keep their children on a schedule so that they are not more than a month behind on their regular vaccines.

What about HPV, influenza and Hep A?

Although we recommend all of these vaccinations and give them to our own kids, they are not required by the State of Illinois, so we have not made them mandatory for remaining a patient at CHP at this time.

What if my child is only missing one or two shots?

We are requiring that our patients be up to date with all of the required vaccinations. These are DTaP, IPV, HIB, PCV13, Hep B, MMR, VZV (Chicken Pox), Tdap, and Menactra vaccines. If you have a question about your child’s immunization status, please call our office.
