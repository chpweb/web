Flu Vaccine Clinics for 2012-2013

In order to accommodate our patients, we will be offering Flu vaccine by appointment during our flu vaccine clinics held on various dates this fall. Those who meet the criteria for high risk by the CDC will be scheduled for our first 2 clinics. FluMist, a nasal flu vaccine, is available for non-high risk children 2 years of age and older. Please tell the scheduler which you prefer for your child when booking your appointment. Only flu shots or Flumist Nasal Spray will be administered at flu clinics. All clinics are dependent upon manufacturer release of vaccines. Once you've scheduled your child's appointment, please make a note of the date and time as reminder calls are NOT performeded the previous day due to the high volume of appointments. Please continue to refer to the website for updates.

FLU VACCINES ARE RECOMMENDED FOR:

  • EVERYONE aged 6 months and older

HIGH RISK CLINIC DATES:

  • Wednesday, September 12
  • Saturday, September 22

High Risk Clinics are designated for:

  • Children diagnosed with any chronic disease affecting the lungs, heart, kidneys, liver, blood or immune system. This includes all children with diabetes and asthma or two episodes of wheezing within the past year.
  • All household contacts of children and adults with chronic disease or immune suppression.
  • All household contacts of children less than 6 months of age.

WELL VISITS AND FLU VACCINE: If your child has a well child check-up in September, October, or November, they can have their flu immunization at that time. There is no need to come to a clinic.

NOTE: Children who are severely allergic to eggs should NOT receive the flu vaccine. Please contact our office with any questions.

BOOSTERS: If your child is 8 years of age or younger and it is his or her first time getting the Flu vaccine, they will need a second dose 4 weeks after the first dose.

RESTRICTIONS: No child will be vaccinated without a scheduled appointment. Flu clinics do not allow time to discuss medical concerns about your child. Please call the office during regular office hours to speak to a nurse or doctor or to receive any other vaccines.
